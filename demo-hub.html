Key Points to Consider

  • These assets are only compatible with Platform. Compatibility with apps such as CS, PGP etc. is on the roadmap.
  • Blueprint automations in the DemoX library can sometimes become outdated due to the biweekly releases from the Blueprint team.
  • This is not a fully built-out application — only a percentage of the app has been configured to allow end-to-end execution for individual case types.
  • These assets are deployed on sandbox environments, so there may be latency issues.

Lessons Learnt

  • If part of a presentation — Allow more than an hour if a Blueprint demo is also on the agenda. More info ▾
    The demo consists of two parts: ideation and the actual software with the omni-channel experience. There is an overlap between a few feature functions, so sufficient time must be allocated to cover both parts effectively.
  • If used as a leave-behind — Book an hour of guided walkthrough with participants. More info ▾
    This ensures participants are familiar with the navigation and know where to click. This session is best conducted in person, with the SC and DemoX team facilitating together.
  • Preparation is key — Factor in time to prepare for the presentation. More info ▾
    Transitions and handovers between presenters are important. If you are adding feature functions on top of the Blueprint, both development time and preparation time must be factored into your planning.

Background

Telstra is a mature account for Pega. They operate on a self-managed cloud with their own COE team and run on a subscription model with no significant rise in case usage.

Tushar Mehta, Pega CE, who joined towards the end of last year, wanted to drive a spike in case-based licensing revenue. He met with Vineet Mehta, Executive, SE & IT | Product & Technology, and asked him to identify the most difficult use case in his area — with the intention of solving it using Pega's case management capabilities. He also wanted Vineet to see the value of Pega not just as a solution for a single use case, but as a design pattern that could be applied across multiple areas within Telstra.

Vineet then engaged his broader team, who identified Device and Care Management as a problem area. His team of architects made it clear that they only wanted Pega to serve as the case management solution, integrating with microservices in the Telstra enterprise layer to enrich the case. They also highlighted that this case is handled by store agents, whose main frustration is having to click a link in Salesforce that opens a ServiceNow application in a separate tab — making it cumbersome to work across two different systems. If Pega could demonstrate that it can work with the microservices and embed the Pega case within Salesforce Desktop, that would be a significant win.

With the support of Tushar's pitch and aligned to key win themes, the official engagement commenced.
